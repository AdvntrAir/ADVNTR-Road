trailheadSummary: |
  This is a week of thresholds. Canyonlands reopened its Needles district as the Babylon Fire finally came under control, and Zion closed its Narrows right on schedule when monsoon storms rolled in — two parks doing exactly what they're supposed to do when conditions demand it. North Cascades went the other direction: two new fires joined a tripled Luna Fire this week, and the backcountry that guide has been sitting on, unpublished, just got harder to recommend without caveats. Yosemite's crowding problem, meanwhile, isn't weather or fire — it's policy. Dropping the reservation system produced exactly the packed, cliffside conditions on the Mist Trail that the system existed to prevent, and nobody in that video chose to be there. The pattern worth naming: some of this week's risk is the mountains doing what mountains do, and some of it is a choice somebody in Washington made in February landing on somebody's ankles in July. Four stories cleared the bar this week; several more — an endangered species habitat rule, a Yellowstone bridge project — didn't, either for timing or for thin sourcing. As containment climbed past 80 percent by mid-July, the district reopened at 8 a.m. Saturday, July 18, along with Highway 211 itself and the surrounding Manti-La Sal National Forest lands.   explorerImpact: |
    Yosemite dropped its day-use reservation system for 2026 after five years of piloting one, betting that 2025 data showed most weekdays had room to spare. June arrived with the park's highest visitation for that month in a decade outside 2016, and by mid-July, video of hikers packed against the railing above Vernal Fall's Mist Trail — a route with a documented history of fatal falls — was circulating nationally. Parking lots fill by 7 a.m. now, and a staff survey found most park employees opposed dropping reservations in the first place. Two California senators have asked Interior to reinstate the system if the season worsens. Nothing has changed procedurally yet, and there's no indication it will before Labor Day.
