### PLANNING CHECKLIST

- [ ] Book Cougar Rock campsite on Recreation.gov - fills within hours of the 6-month window; set a calendar reminder

- [ ] Route around the full 2026 Ohanapecosh closure - avoid the southeast quadrant for camping and loop trail configurations.

- [ ] Download offline maps for entire park - Gaia GPS or AllTrails with Pacific Northwest tile packs downloaded before departure

- [ ] Confirm rig waste tank capacity against planned stay length - no dump stations operational inside the park for 2026

- [ ] Identify gateway town dump station en route (Packwood for south/east approach; Enumclaw for northeast approach)

- [ ] Verify tunnel clearance on planned approach road: avoid Stevens Canyon Road if height exceeds 12' 6". Avoid SR-123 if height exceeds 13' 1".

- [ ] Check Fryingpan Creek Bridge construction delay status if planning Sunrise access (20-30 min one-lane delays active through 2026/2029)

- [ ] Confirm Sunrise Road open status for planned travel dates (opens early July, closes late September - dates shift annually)

- [ ] Stock up on groceries, propane, and fuel before entering mountain roads - options thin and prices high in Ashford/Elbe; Eatonville, Puyallup, or Enumclaw are the right stops

- [ ] Pack microspikes if traveling in June or early July - high-elevation trails retain packed snow and ice well into summer

- [ ] Pack windproof layers regardless of valley temperature - summit-adjacent elevations at Sunrise and Fremont Lookout can drop 30-40°F below lower elevations

### KEY RESOURCES

**Resource: NPS Mount Rainier - Plan Your Visit**

- **URL:** https://www.nps.gov/mora/planyourvisit/index.htm

- **What it's for:** Current road conditions, campground status, closures, alerts, visitor center hours

- **When to use it:** 48 hours before departure and morning of travel - conditions change

**Resource: Recreation.gov - Mount Rainier Campgrounds & Wilderness Permits**

- **URL:** https://www.recreation.gov/permits/4675317

- **What it's for:** Cougar Rock reservations, fee verification, backcountry permit applications, site maps

- **When to use it:** 6 months before planned summer dates; track walk-ups and rolling FCFS cancellations.

**Resource: WSDOT - SR-165 / Fairfax Bridge Closure Updates**

- **URL:** https://wsdot.wa.gov/travel/real-time-travel-information/traffic-alerts

- **What it's for:** SR-165 closure status, any updates to northwest park access

- **When to use it:** Before planning any visit involving the Carbon River or Mowich Lake corridor

**Resource: Gaia GPS**

- **URL:** https://www.gaiagps.com/

- **What it's for:** Offline topographic maps for use inside the park where cell service is unavailable

- **When to use it:** Download Pacific Northwest tile packs before leaving civilization

Rainier rewards preparation more than most parks in the Pacific Northwest - not because it's technically complex to visit, but because the window of optimal conditions is short, the parking is brutal when you get it wrong, and the infrastructure gaps in 2026 (no dump stations, one major campground closed) punish assumptions.

The checklist above handles the logistics. The only thing left is the timing. If you arrive before 7:00 AM, the park is yours. If you arrive after 9:00 AM in July or August, you're in line. Everything else is secondary to that arithmetic.
