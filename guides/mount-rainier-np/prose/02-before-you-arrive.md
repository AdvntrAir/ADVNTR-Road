### GATEWAY TOWNS

**Gateway Town: Packwood**

- **Role:** Primary big-rig base camp; southeastern and eastern entry anchor; full restocking hub

- **Distance to park:** ~12 miles to Stevens Canyon / Ohanapecosh entrance; ~18 miles to Paradise via WA-123

- **Key services:** Fuel (mid-sized RV capable - big rigs should fuel on I-5 before heading east), grocery basics, propane, craft dining

- **RV parks or campgrounds near this town:**
  - **Name:** Packwood RV Park & Campground
  - **Hookups:** Full hookups (30-amp and 50-amp electric, water, sewer) available across 77 dedicated sites.
  - **Pull-through:** Yes, pull-through layouts can accommodate rigs up to 40 feet in length.
  - **Max length:** 40 ft max vehicle capacity limit.
  - **Notes:** Wooded setting on nearly 8 acres. Paved and gravel level sites, clean restrooms, laundry facilities ($1.75 per load), and a basic dog walk area. Note that wireless internet infrastructure is highly unstable and frequently drops connectivity under heavy summer loads.

- **Restaurants worth noting:** Packwood Brewing Co. - craft beer and post-hike meals, locally recommended.

- **What travelers do here:** Use as full-hookup base camp for multi-day park visits. Store the rig, drive uncoupled into the park.

- **Editorial note:** Packwood is the honest answer for anyone pulling a large fifth wheel or a Class A. The park's interior roads and campgrounds are not built for 40-footers. Set up here, unhook, and drive in. The Brewing Co. is a legitimate stop - not just because there's nothing else nearby.

**Gateway Town: Ashford**

- **Role:** Immediate staging town at Nisqually Entrance (southwest); gear and last-minute supplies

- **Distance to park:** Less than 1 mile to the Nisqually Entrance gate

- **Key services:** Limited - small general stores only, expensive; primary gear stop is Whittaker Mountaineering

- **RV parks or campgrounds near this town:**
  - **Name:** Elbe RV / commercial options near Elbe (~5 miles west of Ashford)
  - **Notes:** Noisy - proximity to Mt. Rainier Scenic Railroad tracks. Functional and level but not recommended as a destination camp. More of an emergency overnight.
  - **Hookups:** Basic water and 30-amp hookups only

- **Restaurants worth noting:** Train car diners in Elbe (novelty, not destination dining). Limited options.

- **What travelers do here:** Last stop before entering the park. Gear adjustments, final water fill if available.

- **Editorial note:** Do not rely on Ashford or Elbe for groceries, fuel at good prices, or propane. Stock up in Eatonville, Puyallup, or further west before you get into the mountains. Once you're past the foothills, prices go up and selection goes down.

**Gateway Town: Enumclaw**

- **Role:** Final major resupply hub for travelers entering via the northeast WA-410 corridor

- **Distance to park:** ~35 miles to the White River Entrance

- **Key services:** Full grocery infrastructure (Safeway), propane refills, standard-priced diesel, hardware

- **RV parks or campgrounds near this town:** Standard regional municipal/fairground options.

- **Restaurants worth noting:** Local casual diners along the main commercial strip.

- **What travelers do here:** Final resupply before climbing into the mountains on WA-410. Fill tanks, dump if needed, stock for 3-5 days.

- **Editorial note:** With Ohanapecosh closed for 2026 and Cougar Rock's dump station offline, Enumclaw takes on outsized importance for northeast corridor travelers. Treat it as your last reliable infrastructure before the park.

**Road approach notes:**

- **WA-706 western approach:** Road conditions deteriorate in the final miles approaching the Nisqually gate - potholed, rough on trailer interiors. Keep speed down.

- **Mather Memorial Parkway (WA-410):** Preferred route for larger towables moving between regions. Better lanes, better surface, more pullout infrastructure.

- **Switchbacks above Longmire → Paradise:** Sharp turns, 6-8% sustained grades. Not appropriate for long truck-and-trailer combinations.

- **Stevens Canyon Road:** Connects Paradise to Ohanapecosh. Beautiful but narrow in sections with a low 12' 6" overhead clearance limit across two historic tunnels.

- **Engine braking on descents:** Sources consistently note significant brake wear on long descents from alpine zones. Downshift early, don't ride the brakes.

**Fuel:**

- Ashford/Elbe prices are meaningfully higher than I-5 corridor.

- Big rigs: Fuel on I-5 before heading east on any route.

- Northeast approach: Enumclaw has standard-priced diesel.

The approach to Rainier requires honest route planning - not because it's technically difficult, but because the roads punish assumptions. The western approach on WA-706 is the most popular and the most punishing for trailers: rough in the final stretch, steep above Longmire, with switchbacks that will eat up the afternoon if you're pushing a rig past thirty feet. High-clearance and tall toy haulers must also map overhead constraints; the interior spine of Stevens Canyon Road pinches down to 12 feet, 6 inches at the tunnels, which will cleanly remove a rooftop AC unit if you aren't paying attention.

If you're coming from the east or moving between park zones, WA-410 along the Mather Memorial Parkway is the better choice - wider, better-maintained, with pullouts that actually work for oversized setups. It's also your gateway to Sunrise, which most travelers underplan for.

Stock up before you get into the mountains regardless of your approach. Ashford has one gear store worth visiting and general store prices that aren't. Packwood is more functional but not a full grocery run. Puyallup, Eatonville, and Enumclaw are where you handle real provisioning.
