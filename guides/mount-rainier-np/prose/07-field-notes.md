### FEES

- **Entrance fee (single vehicle, 7-day):** $30

- **Entrance fee (motorcycle, 7-day):** $25

- **Entrance fee (per person, walk-in/bike):** $15

- **Annual park pass:** $55 Mount Rainier Annual Pass

- **America the Beautiful Pass accepted:** Yes - speeds entry at gate.

- **Cashless:** Yes. All entry points, including Nisqually and White River gates, are completely cashless. Ensure you have a debit or credit card handy.

- **Free entrance days 2026:** Valid for U.S. residents only: President's Day (Feb 16), Memorial Day (May 25), Flag Day (June 14), Independence Day Weekend (July 3-5), NPS Birthday (Aug 25), Constitution Day (Sept 17), Theodore Roosevelt's Birthday (Oct 27), Veterans Day (Nov 11).

- **Campground fees:** ~$20/night (Cougar Rock, White River)

- **Dump station fee:** N/A - no internal dump options exist.

- **Backcountry permit fee:** $6.00 flat non-refundable application fee + $12.00 per person, per night camping fee (Youth 15 and under are free).

### PERMITS

- **Backcountry permit required:** Yes (Mandatory for all overnight backcountry zones and the Wonderland Trail).

- **Platform:** Recreation.gov

- **Reservation window:** Early Access Lottery runs from early February to early March. General remaining inventory goes live in late April.

- **Walk-up availability:** Approximately one-third of all backcountry campsites are explicitly reserved for first-come, walk-up requests at Wilderness Information Centers. Must apply in person one day prior to start date.

- **Bear canister required:** Highly recommended in alpine/cross-country zones where trees are unavailable. Food hanging lines (12 ft high, 10 ft out) mandatory at designated camps.

- **Bear canister rental available:** Borrow hooks available for free across major park Wilderness Information Centers.

### CELL SERVICE

- **General coverage:** Essentially none

- **Areas with service:** Intermittent weak signal at Paradise Visitor Center area (Verizon reported most reliable). Potentially minimal signal at some pullouts above Longmire.

- **Areas without service:** Sunrise (zero). Ohanapecosh (zero). White River / Cougar Rock (zero). All river valley corridors (zero). WA-410 mountain approach (minimal to none).

- **Recommended offline tools:** Gaia GPS (download Pacific Northwest tile packs), AllTrails offline, NPS app offline download, downloaded maps via Google Maps / Apple Maps before departure.

- **Emergency contact:** Call 911 for all life-safety emergencies inside the park.

### SEASONAL REALITIES

**Peak season:** July through August
What does peak look like: Paradise and Sunrise parking lots filled by 8:00-8:30 AM on clear days. WA-706 approach backed up at the Nisqually gate on Saturday/Sunday mornings. No timed entry in 2026 means 2019-era gridlock has returned. Campsite reservations at Cougar Rock fill within hours of Recreation.gov window opening. White River is first-come and gone early.

**Shoulder season:** June and September
June: Higher elevation areas (Sunrise, upper Skyline Trail) may still have significant snowpack. Wildflowers begin at lower elevations. Fewer crowds, lower parking pressure.
September: Clearer skies, diminished crowds, wildflower season ending but fall color beginning at lower elevations. Best month for the combination of conditions and manageable crowds. Sunrise Road begins closing for the season in late September to early October.

**Off-season:** October through May
Sunrise Road closes seasonally (typically October). Paradise can be accessed through winter for snowshoeing and backcountry skiing when weather permits, but upper road sections close. Longmire remains accessible year-round and is the low-elevation fallback.

A few things that will save you time at Rainier in 2026, in plain terms.

No timed entry. The park dropped its vehicle reservation pilot entirely for this season. You don't need a corridor pass, a dated ticket, or anything beyond your entrance fee or your America the Beautiful pass. What you do need is to arrive early or arrive late - because the lots at Paradise and Sunrise fill by 8:30 AM on clear summer days, and this year there's no cap on how many vehicles can try.

No dump stations inside the park. Not at Cougar Rock, not anywhere. If you're in an RV, you're managing waste at the gateway towns - Packwood has full hookups, Enumclaw has dump access. Build that into your stay length calculations before you drive in.

Cell service is a yes/no question with one answer: no. The intermittent signal at Paradise Visitor Center is the exception, not the baseline. Download your maps, download your permits, download your trail data before you leave the gateway town. You'll be offline from the moment you drop below the treeline.
