### Campgrounds

---
**Campground: Cougar Rock Campground**

- **Location/Region:** Paradise Alpine Corridor, southwest zone - WA-706 near Longmire

- **Type:** NPS

- **Open dates:** Late May through October

- **Reservations:** Recreation.gov - mandatory during peak summer season

- **Reservation window:** Up to 6 months in advance

- **Fills:** Within hours of window opening during summer

- **Sites total:** 179

- **Price per night:** ~$20

- **Hookups:** None - dry camping only

- **Max vehicle length:** ~30 ft max; tight turns and low-hanging branches in loops make 35+ ft rigs impractical

- **Dump station:** CLOSED - entire 2026 season. No dump or potable water fill infrastructure operational.

- **Showers:** No

- **Pets:** Yes, leashed - campground areas only. Dogs not permitted on park trails.

- **Pet notes:** Pets restricted to paved surfaces and campground. No trail access.

- **Website:** https://www.nps.gov/mora/planyourvisit/cougar-rock-campground.htm

- **Booking URL:** https://www.recreation.gov/camping/campgrounds/232464

- **Google Maps URL or coordinates:** 46.7590° N, 121.7982° W

- **Verified date:** June 2026

- **Editorial note:** Cougar Rock is the interior park option for smaller rigs - Sprinters, Class C units, vans, and trailers under 30 feet. Heavily timbered sites with fire rings give it a genuine camp feel, not a parking lot. The 2026 catch: the dump station and water fill are shut down for the full season. Arrive with empty tanks and full water - there is no workaround inside the park this year.

**Campground: White River Campground**

- **Location/Region:** Northeast zone - Sunrise corridor, White River valley

- **Type:** NPS

- **Open dates:** Late June through late September

- **Reservations:** First-come, first-served

- **Reservation window:** N/A

- **Fills:** Early morning on summer weekends - arrive before 9 AM

- **Sites total:** 88

- **Price per night:** ~$20

- **Hookups:** None - dry camping only

- **Max vehicle length:** Tight, rocky layout and narrow interior loops make this completely unsuitable for larger trailers or motorhomes. Hard practical length limit is 24 feet max.

- **Dump station:** No

- **Showers:** No

- **Pets:** Yes, leashed - campground only

- **Pet notes:** No trail access for pets

- **Website:** https://www.nps.gov/mora/planyourvisit/white-river-campground.htm

- **Booking URL:** N/A - first-come

- **Google Maps URL or coordinates:** 46.9004° N, 121.6404° W

- **Verified date:** June 2026

- **Editorial note:** The closest camp to Sunrise, set along the river with sites that feel earned. The road network accessing the loops is narrow enough to turn around mid-sized rigs - don't attempt it in anything past 24 feet. First-come means showing up and hoping. Midweek is your best play.

### OUTSIDE-THE-PARK OPTIONS

**Campground: Packwood RV Park & Campground**

- **Location/Region:** Packwood, WA - gateway town, southeast corridor

- **Type:** Private

- **Open dates:** Open year-round

- **Reservations:** Recommended via direct online portal or phone call.

- **Fills:** Consistently during peak summer weekend hiking windows

- **Sites total:** 77 dedicated RV pads across an 8-acre wooded boundary.

- **Price per night:** Standard private rates apply (ranging based on seasonal full-hookup demands).

- **Hookups:** Full hookups available at all main sites (30/50 amp electrical service, water, and direct sewer lines).

- **Max vehicle length:** Pull-through capabilities comfortably accommodating setups up to 40 feet.

- **Dump station:** Yes (integrated directly within full hookup sites)

- **Showers:** Yes, hot facilities available on-site

- **Pets:** Allowed on-site under standard leash boundaries.

- **Google Maps URL or coordinates:** 46.6065° N, 121.6710° W

- **Verified date:** June 2026

- **Editorial note:** For any rig over 30 feet, Packwood is the answer. Full hookups, level gravel sites, Wi-Fi that handles basic messaging (though it bogs down during evening peaks), and enough turnaround room to not hold your breath maneuvering. The tradeoff is commuting into the park rather than waking up in it - but that's a reasonable deal when Cougar Rock's dump station is dark and Ohanapecosh is closed for the season.

---

**2026 campground landscape summary:** The park's internal camping infrastructure is significantly degraded this season. Cougar Rock is open but has no dump or water fill. Ohanapecosh is closed entirely. White River is first-come only. Mowich Lake is backcountry-only, inaccessible by vehicle. For RV travelers - especially large rigs - the honest answer is outside the park in commercial full-hookup parks (Packwood primary) with commute access.

**Rig size stratification:**

- **35+ ft Class A / fifth wheels:** Packwood RV base camp. Do not attempt interior park campgrounds.

- **24-30 ft Class C / travel trailers:** Cougar Rock (tight, but navigable). Avoid White River.

- **Under 24 ft / Sprinters / van builds:** Full range - Cougar Rock, White River, forest service land outside the park.
