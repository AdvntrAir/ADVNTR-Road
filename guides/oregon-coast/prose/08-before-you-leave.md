This corridor rewards preparation more than most West Coast drives, mostly because it punishes improvisation. Get the tide charts downloaded, get the reservations locked in early, and go in knowing the fee structure changed recently enough that older advice about it is already wrong in places. None of that diminishes the drive. It just means the people who show up prepared have a noticeably better trip than the people who don't.

### PLANNING CHECKLIST

- [ ] Download offline maps and trail files (Gaia GPS or AllTrails) for the full 363-mile corridor - cell coverage disappears south of Yachats and inside forested campground loops

- [ ] Save current NOAA tide charts offline - Bandon's tide pools and Thor's Well require specific tide windows for safe access

- [ ] Reserve Fort Stevens and/or Tillicum Beach well in advance - both fill months out in peak summer

- [ ] Buy an Oregon State Parks annual parking permit ($60 resident / $75 non-resident) if you're stopping at multiple day-use areas - cheaper than paying per visit at Fort Stevens and Bullards Beach

- [ ] Purchase an Oregon Shellfish License at myodfw.com before dropping any crab nets or digging clams - required for anyone 12 and up

- [ ] Buy the $5 Forest Service day pass (or a Northwest Forest Pass) before parking at Yaquina Head or an Oregon Dunes NRA staging area

- [ ] Reserve an ATV or dune buggy rental in Florence ahead of time for summer weekends - walk-ins aren't reliable in peak season

- [ ] Call ahead to confirm The Crazy Norwegian's current operating status before counting on it for a Port Orford meal stop

- [ ] Carry leveling blocks or SnapPads - root heave is common on older state park asphalt pads

- [ ] Pack windproof rain layers and rubber-soled, high-traction footwear for wet intertidal rock

### KEY RESOURCES

**Resource: TripCheck (ODOT)**

- **URL:** https://tripcheck.com

- **What it's for:** Real-time lane restrictions, landslide construction delays, and emergency cliffside erosion closures

- **When to use it:** Every morning before pulling out onto US-101

**Resource: ODFW Electronic Licensing System**

- **URL:** https://myodfw.com

- **What it's for:** The Oregon Shellfish License required for crabbing and clamming

- **When to use it:** A few days before reaching Charleston, Bandon, or any bay you plan to crab or clam

**Resource: Oregon State Parks - Day-Use Parking Permits**

- **URL:** https://stateparks.oregon.gov

- **What it's for:** Understanding which parks now charge day-use fees, and buying the annual permit if it makes sense for your trip

- **When to use it:** Before departure - this is the single most out-of-date piece of information in older trip reports about this corridor

**Resource: Oregon Dunes NRA (Siuslaw National Forest)**

- **URL:** https://www.fs.usda.gov/siuslaw

- **What it's for:** Day pass purchase, OHV staging area locations, and ATV permit requirements

- **When to use it:** Before staging any dune ride out of Florence or Reedsport
