Most travelers enter Olympic through Port Angeles, Forks, or the southern coast, but the first useful decision happens before the entrance station. The peninsula roads are scenic and slow. Distance on the map rarely tells the full truth.

Build your first day around one major move. If you arrive late, sleep near your entry point and start the park the next morning. Summer weekends reward early departures, flexible meal plans, and a willingness to stop before everyone is tired.

Port Angeles is the most forgiving northern hub. Forks is the west-side reset between rain forest and coast. Sequim and Port Townsend are useful before the road narrows into the peninsula rhythm. Amanda Park makes sense when Lake Quinault or the southern approach is the point.
