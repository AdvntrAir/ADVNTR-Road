The Olympic coast is not a casual beach stroll with better branding. It is rugged, tidal, and often slower than expected. Check tide tables before committing to headland routes, and leave room for fog, wind, and wet gear.

Kalaloch is the simplest coastal base. It is not secluded, but it is practical, and practicality matters when the next drive is longer than it looks.

Ruby Beach is the central coast's strongest quick-payoff stop: sea stacks, driftwood, and enough drama to justify the detour without turning the whole trip into a backpacking itinerary.
