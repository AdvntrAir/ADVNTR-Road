The rain forest is where Olympic stops behaving like a normal national park itinerary. The Hoh is famous for good reason: the scale, the green, and the river all feel oversized. It also absorbs crowds imperfectly, especially in peak season.

Quinault is the quieter southern counterweight. It does not replace the Hoh, but it changes the pace. The roads, lake, lodge infrastructure, and forest edges make it easier to build a lower-pressure day.

Bring rain gear regardless of the forecast. This side of the peninsula does not need permission from your weather app.
