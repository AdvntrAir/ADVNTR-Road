North Cascades is the off-grid stress test for RVs and vans. Come with house batteries fully charged, tanks empty, and full acceptance that the corridor is a cellular dead zone from Marblemount to Mazama. The driving itself demands attention — sustained six percent grades, narrow shoulders, and enough curves that cruise control isn't really an option. Downshift, don't ride the brakes, and give yourself more time than the mileage suggests.

Tent campers should look hard at Colonial Creek South — old-growth forest floor sites steps from the water, with morning paddle launches before the day-use crowd arrives. Day-trippers and lodge-based travelers can sweep the whole SR-20 corridor from Seattle to Winthrop or back, hitting Diablo Lake Vista Point and Washington Pass Overlook without committing to a single hike, though the driving alone takes most of a day done properly.

The bucket-list detour for anyone with extra time is Stehekin — a roadless village at the head of Lake Chelan, reachable only by ferry, floatplane, or a multi-day hike. Leave the rig at secured parking in Chelan or Fields Point and board the Lady of the Lake. One heads up for 2026: North Cascades Lodge, the valley's only hotel-style operator, is closed this summer for fire and flood recovery — no rooms, fuel, laundry, or showers in Stehekin this season. The ferry and shuttle are still running. Plan to camp if you go, not to check into a room.

Minimal light pollution along the high passes also makes this one of the better stargazing stops in the state — worth a late-night pullout if the sky's clear.
