Every campground on this corridor is dry camping — no hookups anywhere in the park complex, and old-growth canopy that makes rooftop solar close to useless. Plan power around generator hours, not sun, and expect the whole west side to be a cell dead zone regardless of which loop you land in.

Newhalem Creek is the safe pick for anything long: the largest campground in the complex, with loops that handle big rigs and a real dump station. Colonial Creek South is the one worth fighting for on Recreation.gov at midnight — private, wooded sites that step straight onto Diablo Lake's jade water — but the internal loop turns are tight, and anything pushing 30-plus feet will have a stressful time backing in. Colonial Creek North is the overlooked sibling across the highway: smaller rigs that can't make South's turns often fit here instead.

Gorge Lake Campground has been closed indefinitely since the 2023 Sourdough Fire, with no reopening date announced — don't plan around it. Goodell Creek fills that gap: open year-round, small, workable for rigs up to about 20 feet. For anything bigger that misses a reservation, the move is outside the park entirely — Forest Service sites near Marblemount, or Harvest Hosts properties in the gateway towns.
