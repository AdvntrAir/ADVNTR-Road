Marblemount is your final west-side resupply for fuel, propane, and baseline groceries before the data blackout. Buffalo Run Restaurant is the only real sit-down meal there, so plan around its hours if you're arriving hungry. Winthrop covers the east side with full-service grocery, fuel, and Rocking Horse Bakery for coffee and pastry.

If you want convenience and don't want to leave Winthrop, Methow Valley Thriftway covers the basics. If you want better prices and a wider selection and don't mind a ten-minute drive, Hank's Harvest Foods in Twisp — family-run, wall-to-wall taxidermy, better produce — is the one locals actually shop at. Neither is wrong; it depends whether you're grabbing dinner or provisioning for a week.

Neither gateway town has big-box pricing. Buy bulk dry goods along I-5 or in the interior valleys before committing to the mountain leg, and don't save every errand for the last stop before the highway climbs into the blackout zone.
