There is no entrance fee here, ever — one of roughly 20 fee-free national parks nationwide, and true for vehicles, motorcycles, and walk-ins alike. That fee-free status has a real exception: the National Forest trailheads just outside the park boundary, including Blue Lake, Rainy Pass, Maple Pass, and Cutthroat Lake, all require a Northwest Forest Pass or America the Beautiful pass for parking. All in-park transactions are cashless.

Cell coverage is essentially nonexistent along the SR-20 interior. Marblemount holds the last reliable signal westbound; the only interior spot with intermittent service is the Washington Pass Overlook platform itself. Download Gaia GPS or AllTrails offline maps before you commit to the drive.

Backcountry travel requires a permit year-round. The process has changed recently: most advance reservations are now issued by email from the Wilderness Information Center (noca_wilderness@nps.gov) after a mandatory online safety video, sent no more than three days before your trip — in-person walk-up pickup is now the exception rather than the default, though roughly 40 percent of permit capacity is still held for walk-up issuance the day of or day before. Bear canisters are required in designated alpine zones and loan free at the Wilderness Information Center.

Several current closures are worth knowing before you go. A park-wide campfire ban took effect June 29, 2026, covering wood, briquettes, and propane fire pits alike. Gorge Lake Campground remains closed indefinitely from 2023 fire damage. Stetattle Creek Trail, Little Beaver Trail, and several backcountry camps remain closed from 2025-2026 fire and flood damage. And while the SR-20 flood and rockslide closure between Colonial Creek and Porcupine Creek fully reopened June 14 after nearly six months shut, this corridor has proven it can close well outside the normal winter window — verify current status before you leave, regardless of what any guide says.

Beyond the winter closure that shuts the high pass from roughly mid-November through late April or May, expect two other risk windows: mid-to-late summer wildfire smoke, and the kind of atmospheric-river flood damage 2026 just demonstrated. Keep a backup route via US-2 and Stevens Pass in mind.
