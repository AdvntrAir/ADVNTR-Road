The highway earns its "American Alps" nickname here. SR-20 climbs through tight switchbacks into a mountain amphitheater where granite spires stand close enough to the road that Liberty Bell Mountain feels less like scenery and more like a wall you're driving past. Washington Pass Overlook is the payoff for anyone who doesn't want to hike for it: a two-tenths-of-a-mile paved loop, fully accessible, ending at a platform that looks straight across the gap at the peak and straight down at the hairpin the highway just carried you through. The parking loop is genuinely built for big rigs.

> Washington Pass regularly runs 20 to 30 degrees colder than the lowland Skagit floor. Bring real layers even for a ten-minute overlook stop.

Blue Lake asks more of you and gives more back. Five miles roundtrip, a steady climb through sub-alpine meadow to a lake beneath the peaks, snow lingering on the trail into July most years. One catch: this trailhead sits on National Forest land just outside the boundary, so the fee-free status doesn't apply. You'll need a Northwest Forest Pass or America the Beautiful pass, or you'll be turning around in a full lot having wasted the morning.
