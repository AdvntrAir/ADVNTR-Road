Treat Marblemount as a checkpoint, not a town to browse. This is where you make the decisions that determine whether the next few days are relaxed or miserable: fuel topped off, propane full, tanks empty, maps downloaded. The Wilderness Information Center here is non-negotiable if you're headed into the backcountry — it's the only place on the west side issuing walk-up permits and loaning bear canisters.

Big-box grocery doesn't exist in either gateway town. Buy heavy provisions in Burlington or Sedro-Woolley before committing to the corridor, not in Marblemount itself.

Winthrop, on the other hand, is worth lingering in. It's dry where Marblemount is damp, sunny where the west side is shaded, with real bakeries and breweries instead of tourist traps. Driving west to east, Winthrop is where the trip becomes a vacation again.
