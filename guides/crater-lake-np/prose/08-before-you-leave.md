A short list, but each item on it is something that's cost other travelers real time or real money.

**Confirm your card works.** The entrance gates are cashless with no exceptions. Discovering a payment issue at the Annie Springs station with a car behind you is a bad start. Buy your pass online in advance if you want to eliminate that transaction entirely.

**Download offline maps before you leave signal behind.** Cell service disappears past the Rim Village parking areas and is essentially nonexistent in the places you'll actually want it — canyon basins, Pinnacles Road, the remote East Rim segments. Gaia GPS, AllTrails Pro, or the NPS app's Crater Lake offline profile. Load it while you still have signal.

**Check the current conditions portal before your trip.** The East Rim Drive construction delays are real in 2026 and intermittent — current status and which segments are actively delayed will be listed at nps.gov/crla/planyourvisit/conditions.htm. Check it the week of your trip, not just when you book.

**The Cleetwood Cove Trail is closed through 2028.** If someone told you to book a boat tour or plan a morning around hiking to the water, that information is out of date. No trail access, no boat tours, until at least summer 2029.

**Do your grocery run in Klamath Falls or Medford.** The one in-park store has everything you forgot to buy at real mountain-markup prices. Buy the heavy groceries, coolers, and tow-vehicle fuel before you start the climb.

**An America the Beautiful Pass is worth buying in advance** if you're hitting more than one federal site this year. Get it at an REI or outdoor co-op before you arrive and skip the gate transaction entirely.

**Pack wool and windproof layers even in high summer.** The rim is at 7,000 to 8,000 feet. A July day on the caldera can be warm at noon and genuinely cold by 5 PM. Most people underpack for this.

**Reserve Mazama Campground the moment your window opens.** July through October slots go in hours after the booking window opens, not days. Set a calendar reminder for your booking date and be ready at the computer.

**Two bookmarks worth having before you go:**

*nps.gov/crla/planyourvisit/conditions.htm* — live road status, facility changes, construction delay updates.

*explorecraterlake.com* — campground reservations, Lodge and Cabin bookings, concessionaire hours.

---

**One more thing before you go**

Crater Lake is one of those places where the gap between a good trip and a great one is almost entirely about pace. The people who leave frustrated are usually the ones who tried to drive the full rim loop, hike Garfield Peak, drive to the Pinnacles, and be back on the road by 2 PM — all in one day. The people who leave wanting to come back are usually the ones who picked two or three things, did them slowly, and sat somewhere without an agenda for at least an hour.

The lake is 1,943 feet deep and six miles across. It took 7,700 years to fill with rain and snowmelt after the volcano that created it collapsed. Whatever you planned to do next can probably wait an extra hour while you sit on the Crater Lake Lodge porch and watch the water change color in the afternoon light. That's not a wasted hour. That's the trip.
